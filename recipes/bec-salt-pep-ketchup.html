Bacon Egg & Cheese

A bodega/deli staple that makes my mouth water even thinking about

Hailing originally from NJ, I can't leave out the crown jewel of all breakfast sandwiches: The bacon egg and cheese. Enter any bagel shop on a weekday morning and you'll overhear at least one other patron order this sandwich, usually with salt pepper and ketchup, usually pronounced all as one word "baconeggandcheesesaltpepperketchup". Though I think this is usually more sought out for it's consistency across any place you might be able to order one and convenience during a morning commute, making it at home can be pretty satisfying. My version is messy as hell, with melty cheese mingling with runny egg yolk and maybe a stray drop of ketchup, but don't you dare eat this with a fork and knife, you heathens.

Ingredients

  • 2 eggs
  • 2 strips bacon
  • bread of choice (I go for an everything bagel)
  • 2 slices American cheese
  • salt (to taste)
  • pepper (to taste)
  • ketchup (you guessed it)

Directions

  1. Toast your bread by desired method to desired toastiness (or don't if you like it untoasted)
  2. Add the bacon to a cold non-stick pan, then cook over medium heat until cooked to your preference.
  3. I like to cook my eggs in the bacon grease for it's flavor but if you don't then drain the grease and melt a tablespoon of butter in the pan, or add the oil of your choice, enough to coat the pan.
  4. Crack in the eggs and cook them how you like them (as mentioned in the description, I'm going with over-easy). Be sure to season immediately with salt and pepper.
  5. When the eggs are maybe halfway cooked top each wit a slice of cheese. If you want to help the cheese melt faster adding a bit of water to the pan and covering it with a lid speeds things up
  6. Once cooked stack the eggs, one on top of the other, on the bottom half of the bagel. Top with bacon, then a swirl of ketchup.
  7. Cut in half so the yolk starts oozing over each half of the sandwich and dig in!
